# Otsuka Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We are committed to creating a collaborative and inclusive environment where all team members can contribute effectively. We pledge to make participation in this project a respectful and professional experience for everyone, regardless of background, identity, or experience level.

## Our Standards

Behaviors that contribute to a positive work environment include:

* Communicating clearly and professionally
* Respecting diverse perspectives and expertise
* Providing and accepting constructive feedback gracefully
* Prioritizing project goals and team success
* Supporting colleagues and fostering collaboration
* Taking responsibility for mistakes and learning from them

Unacceptable behaviors include:

* Inappropriate language, imagery, or unwelcome advances
* Disrespectful comments, personal attacks, or trolling
* Harassment in any form, public or private
* Sharing confidential information without authorization
* Any conduct that undermines a professional work environment

## Our Responsibilities

Project maintainers and leadership are responsible for upholding these standards and taking appropriate action when violations occur. This includes the authority to remove, edit, or reject contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct, and to restrict access for contributors who engage in inappropriate, harmful, or unprofessional behavior.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project-related spaces, including repositories, communication channels, meetings, and events. It also applies when representing the project or Otsuka in public forums, whether online or in person.

## Enforcement

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it to your project leadership or HR contact. All reports will be handled confidentially and investigated promptly. The team will take appropriate action based on the circumstances, ensuring fairness and respect for all parties involved.

Project maintainers who fail to uphold or enforce this Code of Conduct may face consequences as determined by project leadership.
